PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

The fabrication of the joint actuator could be achieved by iterations of rapid-prototyping. It involves:

  • Choosing the actuator mechanism.
  • Motor Sizing (required output speed and torque).
  • Conceptualizing with sketches and cardboard-mockups.
  • Prototyping with aluminums, plastics, and hand tools.

The primary embedded coding was realized with TI microcontrollers. The detailed works include:

  • Setting the interrupt priorities of the peripherals.
  • Clock configurations.
  • Finite State Machine for SCI real-time communication.

The product's key feature is to figure out the Initial Pose Detection of the BLDC motor without using the hall sensor. This was achieved by:

  • Pre-define the variable of the index pin of the Optical Encoder.
  • As powering up the actuator system (and encoder), let the wearer swing the arm until hitting the index pin.
  • As soon as hitting the index pin, the mechanical position is refreshed with the corrected position.
  • The electrical angle is also updated according to the pole-pair number of the BLDC motor.
