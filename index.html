Hubert Kim, Ph.D.

Welcome to my personal webpage.
I'm a haptic researcher who earned my Ph.D. degree from the Assistive Robotics Laboratory (ARL) at Virginia Tech. With Dr. Alan Asbeck, we established the hardware robotic laboratory, where I led the Arm Haptic Feedback team throughout the R&D process unto measuring the scientific findings.

The objective of my research is to understand the characteristics of human-robot interaction through physical touch. My research endeavor began with analyzing hardware requirements for the wearable robot to deliver transparent torque. The development of the custom-made actuator was inevitable to discover the minimum requirements for the human perception during the robot-mediated training. Based on the perceptual boundaries, I analyzed the bandwidth the human arm-robot system can handle.

Previously, I researched in Dynamics Systems Laboratory at NYU Tandon under the supervision of Dr. Maurizio Porfiri. My primary role was to apply Signal Processing techniques on smart materials, specifically Piezoelectric and Ionic Polymer Metallic Composite (IPMC) such that we could characterize electromechanical property of the smart material and validate the physics-based models.
